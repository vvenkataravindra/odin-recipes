Vankaay Koora

Description

What is Gutti Vankaya Curry? Gutti vankaya curry is a classic and delicious Andhra style curry made with small or baby eggplants, peanuts, onions and spices. It is nothing but stuffed baby eggplants with a special peanut masala or filling and cooked in the peanut sauce. Gutti vankaya means “whole eggplants” and it is a way of cooking where the veggies are stuffed with spices and cooked whole. So you will need to cook them whole without chopping them into pieces. Choose small and same sized eggplants to ensure even cooking.

    Ingredients:
  • 12 Small Brinjals / Eggplants
  • 1-2 Slit Green Chillies
  • 2-3 tbsp Oil
  • 1-2 tbsp Coriander Leaves
  • 1 Dry Red Chilli
    Steps to be followed:
  1. To prepare the filling, peel and chop onion into pieces and set them aside.
  2. Heat a teaspoon of oil in a wide pan or wok. Add cloves, cinnamon stick, coriander seeds and peanuts and let them fry over a low-medium flame until they are browned.
  3. Add one dried red chilli to the peanuts and saute for another few seconds.
  4. Take them out of the pan and set aside to cool completely.
  5. In the same pan, add a tablespoon of oil.
  6. Add slit green chillies and chopped onion to the pan and saute them until they are transparent and light pink.
  7. Add desiccated coconut or grated dry coconut to the sauteed onions and give it a stir. You can also use fresh coconut.Let it cool.
  8. Combine the roasted peanuts + spices, sauteed onions, tamarind, turmeric powder, ginger, garlic, jaggery and salt in a blender or mixer jar.Add a little bit of water to it and blend until you get a smooth and thick paste.
  9. Wash the baby eggplants or brinjal and make a plus (+) slit on the bottom of the eggplant. Keep one end intact. Carefully stuff them with the ground mixture and set them aside.
  10. Heat 2-3 tablespoons of oil in a pan to cook the brinjals. Gently drop the stuffed brinjals into the pan and give it a mix to coat them evenly.
  11. Pour one cup of water and mix well. Cover the pan with a lid and cook on a medium-low flame until brinjals are soft and cooked. Keep stirring in between and add more water to prevent it from burning
  12. Once the brinjals are cooked, add the chopped coriander leaves and mix. Add more water to adjust the curry consistency.
